Women Of The World

Website Redesign

Case Study

Women of the World is an Non-For-Profit that looks to empower and help women that have been forcibly displaced in society from every corner of the world. The site has a very colorful design that looks to show it’s multi-cultural design. At first glance, it does make it hard to take in the information - so our mission is to maintain that warmth and attract the eyes around the page but all the while allow for the information to be easily absorbed.

Project Overview

Bootcamp Group Project

Timeline- 2 Weeks

User Insight

Users need a way to easily find volunteer opportunities where they can connect with others and feel like they are is making a difference. It is imperative that the site offers a strong message, clean and easy to navigate layout, and clear and direct information.

Problem

Through our research we discovered that most users found the website unappealing in regard to navigation, interface, and functionality. Therefore, we believe that people are being driven away from supporting the cause because the site doesn’t offer a strong message or design that pulls people in and connects directly with their mission.

Solution

Our goal is to reimagine the message and the layout of information across multiple pages in a way that benefits the user and helps them to connect with that data more personally.

Website Analysis

We conducted an analysis of Women of the World's current website as the initial step in our redesign effort. Despite the commendable objective of the non-profit organization, the message was not effectively conveyed through the current web design. The organization's mission statement holds great significance; however, it was not immediately apparent to users on how they could support the cause.

Website Rebrand

We essentially wanted a website that has a consistent theme, in regards to color-scheme/pallet and functionality. We wanted our website have more of a multi-cultural vibe to it, so we chose some skin color tones- which give it a softer, neutral tone. By using clip-art images of women, we were able to add a sense of unity and rhythm throughout the pages. We hoped to have our users more engaged doing so.
